Contact Form

The PHPMailer, a full-featured email production and transfer class for PHP, is now used in the contact form.

How to set up a working contact form

Do below changes in "formmailer/mailer.php" file to receive mail for the form filled in contact.html

Line 6: In place of "xxxxxxxxxxxx" add your AWS or email username.
Line 7: In place of "xxxxxxxxxxxx" add your AWS or email password.
Line 8: In place of "example@domain.com" add your from email id.
Line 9: In place of "Domain Person Name" add your from name via which name you want to send email.
Line 11: In place of "example@domain.com" add your to email id where you want to receive form lead details.
Line 12: In place of "example@domain.com" add your cc email id if in case you want one lead copy to be receive on another email ID too.
Line 13: In place of "example@domain.com" add your bcc email id if in case you want one lead copy to be receive on another email ID too.
Line 14: In place of "Contact form" add your subject line as per your requirement.
Line 16: In place of "email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com" you can add your own host.
Line 17: In place of "xxx" you can change it to your port setting.
Line 19: In place of "EduKids - Thank you for contacting us!" you can change it to your confirmation email subject line.

Create your own confirmation email message

Do below changes in "mailer.php" file to customize confirmation email message.

Line 142: Add full logo URL.
Line 185, 189 and 193: Add your confirmation email message.
